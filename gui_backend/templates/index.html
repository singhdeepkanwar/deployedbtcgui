Classification of Brain Tumor using Efficient-ResidualNet

Abstract

In this study, multiscale feature fusion of Efficient-Residual Net is proposed by effectually amalgamating features from two Deep Convolution Neural Network (DCNN) models for improved classification results on brain tumors with similar morphology. The skip connections in ResNet50 highly reduces the chances of vanishing gradient problem by allowing the gradient to flow through the alternate skipped path. With the commencement of skip connections, higher number of layers can be introduced in the network without overfitting problem. This leads to learning of higher number of features during the training process without any losses. In addition, EffficientNetB0 uses a compound scaling coefficient for uniformly scaling the networks dimensions such as depth, width, and resolution. The hyper tuning of top layers of different pre-trained models, addition of Global Average Pooling (GAP) layer, Dropout layer and Fully Connected (FC) layer with SoftMax classifier increases overall proficiency and also diminishes overfitting problem. The hybrid network is tested on two internet repository datasets viz. Kaggle and BraTS 2018 and Real-time dataset from Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education & Research (PGIMER), Chandigarh.

Algorithm:

  • Input:List of well annotated MR slices.

  • Output:Classification model along with predictions
    • Step1: Import all the 2-D slices with labels and append them into python lists.
    • Step2: Resizing all the MR slices to a size of 256*256.
    • Step3: Converting all the resized slices and supporting labels into NumPy arrays.
    • Step4: Dividing the dataset into training and testing sets comprising of 60% dataset for training & 40% dataset for testing and perform one-hot encoding on labels.
    • Step5: Train the model with training dataset.
    • Step6: Test the model and get predictions performance.

Experiments Performed:

  • Experiment 1: Classification on Kaggle Dataset consisting of Glioma tumor, meningioma tumor, pituitary tumor, and no tumor.
  • Experiment 2: Classification on BrATs Dataset consisting of LGG and HGG.
  • Experiment 3: Classification on Real time dataset collected from PGIMER, Chandigarh, India consisting of Glioblastoma multiforme and Oligodendroglioma.
